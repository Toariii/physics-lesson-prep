# Physics Audit

## Universal Model And Calculation Audit

- Identify the system, boundary, reference frame, sign convention, assumptions, and approximation regime.
- Define every symbol before use. Check algebra, arithmetic, dimensions, units, prefixes, significant figures, signs, vector directions, and order of magnitude.
- Test limiting cases and compare with a known benchmark or defensible physical intuition.
- Keep the question, diagram, solution, final answer, and marking guidance mutually consistent.
- Distinguish the ideal model from the real system and state where the model stops being valid.
- Independently solve every numerical original or adapted question; do not inherit an answer after changing data, wording, geometry, or assumptions.
- Assign confidence exactly as `confirmed`, `provisional`, or `unresolved`; every unresolved physics point blocks release and must be named.

## Mechanics And Rigid-Body Audit

For every free-body diagram, name the object, include only interactions acting on that object, and verify force directions. Friction opposes relative or impending surface motion, not automatically velocity. Check axes, components, constraints, and whether equilibrium or acceleration is supported. Use the stable teaching sequence: choose the object -> isolate it -> identify external interactions -> draw one force per interaction -> choose axes -> resolve components -> write constraints and equations -> check signs and limiting behavior.

For rigid-body motion, state the reference frame, body or system, constraints, generalized coordinates, sign convention, and rigid-body assumptions. Check angular velocity and angular acceleration directions. Audit relative velocity and relative acceleration, including centripetal and tangential terms. State instantaneous-center limits explicitly: the instantaneous-center method is a velocity relation only; the center may be undefined or at infinity, and it is not an acceleration shortcut.

Check rolling no-slip constraints and the frames in which they apply. Define moment of inertia, its axis, and units. Use the parallel-axis theorem only when the axes are parallel and the displacement is measured from the center-of-mass axis. State the system boundaries for energy and angular momentum. Distinguish planar from three-dimensional rotation; verify cross products and right-hand-rule directions.

## Circuits And Electromagnetism Audit

Verify nodes, branches, junctions, polarity, meter placement, open and closed paths, and series/parallel relations. Separate conventional current from electron motion. Apply Kirchhoff rules with declared current and loop conventions. For fields and induction, check charge sign, velocity, field, force, right-hand rules, and Lenz's-law opposition. Determine topology by traced connections, never by visual proximity.

## Waves, Optics, And SHM Audit

Distinguish particle motion from propagation. Check phase, period, frequency, wavelength, path difference, and boundary conditions. In optics, verify the normal, focal points, object and image properties, geometry, and scale status. In SHM, cross-check displacement, velocity, acceleration, restoring force, energy, and phase at key positions.

## Thermal, Fluids, Nuclear, And Modern Physics Audit

Separate temperature, heat, internal energy, and power; verify absolute temperature and energy-transfer sign conventions. State steady, incompressible, non-viscous, or other fluid assumptions before ideal relations. Balance nuclear equations by charge and nucleon number, and distinguish activity, count rate, dose, and energy. In modern physics, state the model's scale and avoid classical claims outside it; identify probabilistic interpretations explicitly.

## Graph And Data Audit

Label each axis with quantity and unit; check scale choice, raw versus transformed data, uncertainty, fit, and theoretical curve. Interpret gradient and intercept with units. Do not infer causation from a graph alone, force a linear model, or conceal rejected data. Check uncertainty propagation and significant figures at the confirmed course depth.

## Diagram Specification And Release Gate

Every diagram specification states purpose, represented system, topology or geometry, labels and units, directions, sign convention, scale status, correspondence with the question and solution, and legibility requirements. If assessed information depends on a diagram, require independent teacher inspection. If any item fails, retain the diagram as `draft`. Generated art remains `draft` until a teacher checks physical correctness, labels, geometry, scale status, accessibility, and intended use.

## Experiment Safety And Feasibility

Confirm apparatus ratings, stable mounting, safe clearances, supervision, and shutdown procedures. Confirm equipment quantity, grouping, reset time, visibility, and whether every learner can observe the decisive event or reading. Identify hazards, persons at risk, controls, residual risk, and local approval needs.

Plan for equipment failure, invisible effects, weak signals, excessive setup time, and unusable data. Provide a simulation, video, teacher demonstration, or prepared dataset fallback when needed. Do not present qualitative observation as quantitative verification. Do not treat a spring-balance reading as net force unless every other force component is modeled and accounted for.

Require local policy and qualified-teacher approval. Treat mains electricity, high voltage, high current, strong magnets, radiation sources, lasers, intense light, pressure or vacuum systems, projectiles, rotating machinery, hot surfaces, flames, cryogens, hazardous chemicals, heavy suspended loads, glass under stress, and work at height as high risk unless a stricter local classification applies.

## Question-Solution-Marking Consistency

Ensure the conditions are sufficient, no undeclared assumption is needed, and the requested result follows from the stated model. Verify prompt, data, diagram, solution, answer, units, tolerances, and marking points one-to-one. Marking must reward the declared reasoning and accept valid alternative solutions. Fully re-solve every variant, original, and adapted problem; do not assume number-only or wording changes preserve the method or answer.

## Cross-Textbook Notation And Depth

Compare notation, definitions, sign conventions, model assumptions, and derivation depth with the confirmed primary textbook. Do not mix conventions silently. State a translation when another source uses different notation or definitions. Authoritative physics may correct a textbook error, but flag the discrepancy for teacher review and preserve the course-facing convention where it remains physically valid.

## Release Labels

Label a package `teacher-review-ready` only when the course, education-design, physics, and intended-use gates all pass. Otherwise label it `draft` and list the exact blockers, including each unresolved confidence item, failed diagram check, safety approval, source issue, or question-solution-marking mismatch. Teacher review remains required for local fit, safety, school policy, and accommodation.
