# Source Validation

## Separate Three Claim Types

- course fact: what the target course requires;
- disciplinary fact: what physics and mathematics establish;
- teaching recommendation: a pedagogical choice for this learner.

Never blur these claim types. Label each recorded claim before judging its evidence.

## A-D Evidence Levels

- A - decisive official/assigned: current official curriculum, specification, assessment document, school requirement, teacher syllabus, or assigned textbook.
- B - authoritative professional: recognized professional body, reputable academic publisher, authoritative same-level reference, or qualified institutional resource.
- C - screened practice: teaching material independently audited for physics accuracy and pedagogical fitness.
- D - lead only: search result, aggregator, informal post, or unverified summary used only to locate stronger evidence.

Lower evidence cannot override higher evidence. Level C requires independent physics and pedagogy audits. Level D only finds sources and never supports a final claim.

## Web Page Verification

An official-looking domain is not enough. Verify the actual department, awarding body, institution, or publisher; page title; course or document code; version and date; originality; completeness; replacement status; region; and relevance to the target claim. Open the original page: search-result snippets are never evidence.

## Official PDF Verification

Open the original PDF, not a snippet. Check cover, institution, document identifier, year, header and footer, version, table of contents, revision history, and replacement notice. Confirm the complete document applies to the target course and year.

## Book And Edition Verification

Verify books using a publisher catalog, WorldCat or another legitimate library catalog, the target course reading list, an author or institution page, or lawful contents and sample pages. A retailer listing, secondhand listing, or unknown PDF is not sole evidence. Record title, author, publisher, ISBN, edition, year, relevant chapter, level, and lawful record used.

## Cross-Validation Rules

For key course versions, assessment changes, required experiments, prerequisites, and weighting, seek at least one decisive source plus a second supporting source when available. If only one official source is available, state that cross-validation is incomplete. Cross-check disciplinary facts against authoritative references when notation or depth may conflict.

## Source Conflict Procedure

Check year, region, examination series, obsolete versions, evidence hierarchy, and local adjustment. Show Source A and Source B separately with institution, version, exact evidence, and evidence level; then record the judgment and required teacher decision. Do not silently choose a source.

## Question Provenance And Copyright

Record source, year or series, edition, item identifier, original or adapted status, official or licensed status, and intended use. Never label an unknown question as a past paper. Do not bypass access controls or reproduce a full unauthorized question bank or textbook. Independently solve and audit every adaptation.

## Research Record

For every source, record claim type, evidence level, title, institution or author, URL or bibliographic record, publication/update or edition year, access date, course/version applicability, exact information used, cross-validation, replacement or conflict risk, verification status, provenance, license, and intended use.

## Course Evidence Package

The Course Evidence Package records course identity and version; textbook and progress; required, recommended, and excluded content; prerequisites; abilities and assessment; official, school, and recommended order; learner evidence gap; sources; unresolved items; and conflicts.

For a university course, keep School Course Boundary separate from Textbook Knowledge Mainline. Offer teacher decisions: A confirm and proceed to S7; B modify order and remain in S6 or return to S5; C research again in S5; D wait in S5 for school material.
